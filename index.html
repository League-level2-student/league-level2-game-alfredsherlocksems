Crazy Chess

by: Alberto

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I enjoy chess and decided that it might be fun to make my own chess game, so here it is! This is a throwback to original chess, so no castling and "en' passant. There are also goofy characters, as well as a surrender button whether you choose to resign on your turn or you are in checkmate, this button is the one you press.

2. What I Learned

I learnt that it's important to have a plan when making a game with multiple components, since code can get quite messy and it's much easier when you're up to date with what's going on and when you're organized.

3. Architecture of My Game

The pieces interact with each other by constantly checking whether they're checking the king, or if they can take an opponent's piece, and make sure it isn't an ally.

4. Technical Implementation

I'm proud of the number of mistakes I made managed to overcome. I had to continuously go back to check what I'd done, finding another mistake which I'd previously missed, but in the end managed to fix it and move foward.

5. Most Challenging Part

Getting the pieces to recognize that they were checking something, and if there was anything blocking. I had to constantly go over this code, since different pieces had multiple problems and I had to try and solve them so at least one piece was functional. In the end, I managed to solve most the problems, and finish the project.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

To take out any bugs, add a working checkmate system, piece customization options, and an optional castling and en' passant system.
